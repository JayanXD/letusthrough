Planting Basics

Start with a good potting mix, provide proper drainage, and water regularly but avoid waterlogging.

Choose seeds or seedlings adapted to your climate and the season.

Use slow-release fertilizer sparingly and monitor the plant for pests.

Repot when the plant outgrows the container. Keep the leaves clean and prune dead parts.

Sunlight: Most vegetables need full sun (6+ hours); many herbs tolerate partial shade.

Watering: A consistent schedule helps seed germination and young growth.

Organic pest control: handpick pests, encourage beneficial insects, and use soapy spray for small infestations.

Soil health: rotate plants and use compost to keep nutrients balanced.
